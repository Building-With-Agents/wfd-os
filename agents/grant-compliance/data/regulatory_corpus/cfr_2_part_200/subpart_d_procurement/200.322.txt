§ 200.322  Domestic preferences for procurements.

(a) As appropriate and to the extent consistent with law, the non-Federal
entity should, to the greatest extent practicable under a Federal award,
provide a preference for the purchase, acquisition, or use of goods,
products, or materials produced in the United States (including but not
limited to iron, aluminum, steel, cement, and other manufactured
products). The requirements of this section must be included in all
subawards including all contracts and purchase orders for work or
products under this award.

(b) FOR PURPOSES OF THIS SECTION:

  (1) "Produced in the United States" means, for iron and steel
  products, that all manufacturing processes, from the initial melting
  stage through the application of coatings, occurred in the United
  States.
  (2) "Manufactured products" means items and construction materials
  composed in whole or in part of non-ferrous metals such as aluminum;
  plastics and polymer-based products such as polyvinyl chloride pipe;
  aggregates such as concrete; glass, including optical fiber; and
  lumber.

APPLICABILITY NOTES (not part of the regulation):

- §200.322 is most directly relevant for procurement of physical goods
  and infrastructure construction materials. For CFA's K8341 grant —
  which is dominated by training-provider contracts and recovery
  consulting contracts (services) — direct §200.322 applicability is
  limited.
- Where CFA does purchase manufactured goods or construction materials
  under K8341 (e.g., laptops or office equipment if any qualify under
  the budget), the preference applies and documentation should record
  that the preference was considered.
- The Build America Buy America Act (BABA, 41 USC 8301 note) layers
  additional iron/steel/manufactured-product/construction-material
  requirements for infrastructure projects funded by federal financial
  assistance. BABA implementation guidance is OMB M-22-11 and
  successor guidance — outside the §200.322 text but relevant to any
  infrastructure scope.
