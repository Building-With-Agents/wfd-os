§ 200.327  Contract provisions.

The non-Federal entity's contracts must contain the applicable provisions
described in Appendix II to this part.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
APPENDIX II SUMMARY (paraphrased, not verbatim — verify against the
official Appendix II text at https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-2/part-200/appendix-Appendix%20II%20to%20Part%20200):

Required contract provisions, by trigger:

1. **Termination clauses**
   - Contracts above $10,000 must include termination for cause and for
     convenience by the non-Federal entity, including the manner of
     termination and the basis for settlement.

2. **Equal Employment Opportunity**
   - Federally-assisted construction contracts must include the E.O.
     11246 EEO clause and reference 41 CFR Part 60-1.4.

3. **Davis-Bacon Act / Copeland "Anti-Kickback" Act**
   - When required by federal program statute, all prime construction
     contracts above $2,000 awarded by recipients and subrecipients must
     include compliance with the Davis-Bacon Act (40 USC 3141-3148) and
     the Copeland "Anti-Kickback" Act (40 USC 3145).

4. **Contract Work Hours and Safety Standards Act (40 USC 3701-3708)**
   - Construction or other public-work contracts above $100,000.

5. **Rights to inventions made under a contract or agreement**
   - Funded research / experimental / developmental / demonstration
     contracts with small business firms or nonprofit organizations
     must comply with 37 CFR Part 401 (Bayh-Dole).

6. **Clean Air Act (42 USC 7401-7671q) and Federal Water Pollution
   Control Act (33 USC 1251-1387), as amended**
   - Contracts and subgrants of amounts in excess of $150,000.

7. **Debarment and suspension (E.O. 12549 and 12689)**
   - All contractor and subcontractor selections must check that the
     party is not listed on the System for Award Management
     (sam.gov) Excluded Parties List. Required for all contracts.

8. **Byrd Anti-Lobbying Amendment (31 USC 1352)**
   - Contractors that apply or bid for an award exceeding $100,000
     must file the required certification.

9. **Procurement of recovered materials**
   - Cross-reference §200.323 — applies to state and political-subdivision
     agencies and their contractors when purchasing items designated
     in EPA CPGs above the $10,000 threshold.

10. **Domestic preferences for procurements (Build America, Buy
    America)**
    - Cross-reference §200.322 and BABA implementing guidance.

11. **Prohibition on certain telecommunications and video surveillance
    services or equipment** (Section 889, FY2019 NDAA, Public Law 115-232,
    implementing regs at 2 CFR 200.216 and 200.471, 48 CFR Subpart 4.21)
    - Recipients and subrecipients are prohibited from obligating or
      expending loan or grant funds to procure or obtain (or extend or
      renew a contract to procure or obtain) certain "covered
      telecommunications equipment or services" produced by entities
      including Huawei, ZTE, Hytera, Hikvision, and Dahua.

APPLICABILITY NOTES (not part of the regulation):

- The "flow-down clauses" requirement means that for each in-scope
  contract, the non-Federal entity must check which Appendix II
  provisions apply (based on contract value, scope, and contract type)
  and ensure those provisions are physically present in the executed
  contract document.
- For CFA's K8341 service contracts, the typically-applicable provisions
  are: termination clauses (always above $10K), debarment/suspension
  (always — sam.gov check), Byrd Anti-Lobbying ($100K+ contracts), and
  Section 889 telecom prohibition (always — flat prohibition regardless
  of dollar amount). Construction-specific (Davis-Bacon, CWHSSA, EEO)
  generally do not apply to CFA's service-only procurements.
- Documentation expectation: for each above-threshold contract, the
  procurement file should include a checklist showing which Appendix II
  provisions apply, which are physically in the contract, and a sam.gov
  exclusion-list check dated near the award.
