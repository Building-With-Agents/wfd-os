§ 200.404  Reasonable costs.

A cost is reasonable if, in its nature and amount, it does not exceed
that which would be incurred by a prudent person under the circumstances
prevailing at the time the decision was made to incur the cost. The
question of reasonableness is particularly important when the
non-Federal entity is predominantly federally-funded. In determining
reasonableness of a given cost, consideration must be given to:

(a) Whether the cost is of a type generally recognized as ordinary and
necessary for the operation of the non-Federal entity or the proper and
efficient performance of the Federal award.

(b) The restraints or requirements imposed by such factors as: sound
business practices; arm's-length bargaining; Federal, state, local,
tribal, and other laws and regulations; and terms and conditions of
the Federal award.

(c) Market prices for comparable goods or services for the geographic
area.

(d) Whether the individuals concerned acted with prudence in the
circumstances considering their responsibilities to the non-Federal
entity, its employees, where applicable its students or membership,
the public at large, and the Federal Government.

(e) Whether the non-Federal entity significantly deviates from its
established practices and policies regarding the incurrence of costs,
which may unjustifiably increase the Federal award's cost.

APPLICABILITY NOTES (not part of the regulation):

- §200.404 is the qualitative rule that complements quantitative thresholds.
  A cost can satisfy a competition / procurement-method / dollar-threshold
  rule and STILL fail the §200.404 reasonableness test if the price was
  not what a prudent person would pay.
- The "prudent person under the circumstances prevailing at the time"
  language matters: reasonableness is judged at the time the decision
  was made, with the information then available. Hindsight is not the
  standard.
- Documentation of reasonableness for above-threshold contracts typically
  takes the form of:
  - Cost or price analysis under §200.324 (independent cost estimate
    before bids; analysis of competing offers; or cost analysis of a
    sole-source proposal's individual cost elements)
  - For sole-source / no-price-competition: market-rate research showing
    comparable services in the geographic area
  - For continuing relationships (e.g., a long-running contractor):
    written rationale that the price tracks comparable arm's-length
    rates, not just past internal rates
- The five factors (a)-(e) are non-exclusive — auditors may consider
  others. The agent should treat the listed factors as the documentation
  baseline, not the ceiling.
- For CFA-specific application: the ~$3K-per-placement rates paid to
  training providers, the AI Engage / Pete Vargo recovery rates, and
  any above-threshold strategic-partner contracts all need a documented
  reasonableness analysis under §200.404. The form of that analysis
  is what §200.324 specifies (cost or price analysis); the test that
  the analysis must support is what §200.404 specifies.
