## Executive Summary

This report analyzes 2,718 job postings collected across the Greater Seattle metropolitan area during Q1 2026 (January - March) through the Computing for All Job Intelligence Engine. The data was sourced from JSearch (aggregating Indeed, LinkedIn, Glassdoor, and ZipRecruiter), USAJobs, and direct employer feeds.

**Key findings:**

- **Python remains the most requested skill** across AI-adjacent roles, appearing in 47% of technical postings
- **Cloud skills (AWS/Azure) demand grew 22%** quarter-over-quarter, now appearing in 34% of postings
- **Junior AI/ML roles are experiencing salary compression**, with entry-level positions clustering in the $75-90K range regardless of employer size
- **"AI Engineer" as a standalone title grew 340%** compared to Q1 2025, displacing "Data Scientist" in many organizations
- **Container orchestration (Kubernetes/Docker)** crossed into the top 10 most-requested skills for the first time

## Methodology

Data was processed through a 6-agent AI pipeline: ingestion from multiple job board APIs, normalization of titles and locations, extraction of skills using LLM-powered analysis, enrichment with employer and industry data, deduplication, and statistical analysis. All data represents unique postings after deduplication.

**Junior salary compression:** The gap between junior and mid-level AI roles is widening. Junior AI engineers in Seattle earn roughly the same as junior software engineers ($78-85K), despite the specialized skill set. This suggests that the market is normalizing AI/ML as a baseline engineering skill rather than a premium specialty.

## Emerging Role Categories

Three role categories showed significant growth in Q1 2026:

### 1. AI Agent Engineer
Roles explicitly requiring experience building autonomous AI agent systems. Most postings reference LLM orchestration, tool use, and multi-step reasoning. Median salary: $145K. Sample size: 23 postings (new category, not tracked in prior quarters).

### 2. AI Infrastructure Engineer
Hybrid roles combining ML platform engineering with cloud infrastructure. Kubernetes, MLOps, and model serving (vLLM, TensorRT) are common requirements. Median salary: $155K.

### 3. Applied AI Analyst
Non-engineering roles that use AI tools for business analysis. Often posted by finance, HR, and operations teams rather than engineering. Lower salary range ($65-90K) but growing rapidly.

## Implications for Workforce Development

1. **Training programs should prioritize Python + SQL + Cloud** — this trifecta covers 70%+ of technical role requirements
2. **Container skills (Docker/Kubernetes) are no longer optional** for mid-level developers
3. **AI/ML fundamentals should be embedded in all CS curricula**, not siloed into specialized tracks
4. **Regional training providers in South King and Snohomish** should focus on SQL and data analysis roles, where salary expectations align better with local cost of living
