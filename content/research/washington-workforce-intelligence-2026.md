## Executive Summary

This report presents a statewide analysis of Washington's tech workforce ecosystem, synthesizing data from the CFA Job Intelligence Engine, Washington State Employment Security Department (ESD), WSAC provider reporting, and coalition employer surveys. The analysis covers all 12 workforce development areas and provides actionable intelligence for workforce boards, training providers, employers, and policymakers.

**Key findings:**

- **Washington has a 23% gap** between employer-demanded tech skills and training program output across community and technical colleges
- **The top 5 skill gaps** (Kubernetes, cloud architecture, AI/ML fundamentals, data engineering, and cybersecurity) represent over 4,200 unfilled positions statewide
- **Rural workforce areas** (Eastern WA, Olympic Peninsula, Southwest WA) show growing tech employer presence but minimal local training pipeline
- **WIOA participant-to-employment conversion** averages 64% statewide, with significant variation by region (48% to 81%)
- **Apprenticeship-based training models** show 2.3x higher retention rates at 180 days compared to traditional classroom-only programs

## Methodology

Data sources include:
- 12,400+ job postings processed through the CFA Job Intelligence Engine (Jan 2025 - Mar 2026)
- ESD quarterly employment data by workforce development area
- WSAC provider outcome reports (2024-2025 program years)
- Direct employer survey data from 47 Washington Tech Workforce Coalition members
- BLS Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics (May 2025 release)

**The fundamental misalignment:** Training providers are still graduating students with Java and HTML/CSS as primary skills, while employers are increasingly asking for Python, cloud infrastructure, and AI/ML fundamentals. This gap is widening, not closing.

## Regional Analysis

### Puget Sound (WDA 1-3: Seattle, Snohomish, Pierce)

- **Postings:** 8,200+ (66% of statewide)
- **Median tech salary:** $128,000
- **Top demand:** Python, Cloud, AI/ML, Kubernetes
- **Training capacity:** 14 community/technical colleges, 4 universities, 6+ bootcamps
- **Gap score:** Moderate — training exists but curriculum alignment is weak

### Eastern Washington (WDA 7-10)

- **Postings:** 890 (7% of statewide)
- **Median tech salary:** $78,000
- **Top demand:** SQL, IT Support, Data Analysis, Cybersecurity
- **Training capacity:** 4 community colleges, 1 university branch
- **Gap score:** Severe — growing employer demand with limited local training

### Southwest Washington (WDA 11-12)

- **Postings:** 620 (5% of statewide)
- **Median tech salary:** $82,000
- **Top demand:** Web Development, SQL, Cloud, DevOps
- **Training capacity:** 2 community colleges
- **Gap score:** High — Vancouver/Clark County has Portland-adjacent demand but limited local supply

## WIOA Participant Outcomes

### Statewide Performance (PY 2024-2025)

| Metric | Target | Actual | Status |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Employment rate Q2 post-exit | 72% | 64% | Below |
| Employment rate Q4 post-exit | 70% | 58% | Below |
| Median earnings Q2 | $7,800 | $6,950 | Below |
| Credential attainment | 65% | 71% | Above |
| Measurable skill gains | 58% | 63% | Above |

**Interpretation:** Washington is meeting credential and skill gain targets but falling short on employment outcomes. This suggests that training quality is adequate but the connection between training completion and employer placement needs strengthening.

## Recommendations

### For Workforce Boards
1. **Invest in real-time intelligence infrastructure** — quarterly reports are insufficient for a labor market moving at AI speed
2. **Prioritize Python/SQL/Cloud in curriculum advocacy** — these three skills cover 70%+ of employer demand
3. **Develop regional specializations** — Eastern WA should focus on IT support and data analysis, not try to replicate Puget Sound's AI/ML programs

### For Training Providers
1. **Add Kubernetes/container fundamentals** to all cloud-track programs immediately — it's now a baseline expectation
2. **Partner with employers for capstone projects** — work-based demonstrations of skill are worth more than certificates
3. **Consider apprenticeship models** — 2.3x retention improvement is too significant to ignore

### For Employers
1. **Engage with local workforce boards** — the talent pipeline exists but needs employer guidance on what skills to prioritize
2. **Consider supervised apprenticeship engagements** — evaluate talent through real project work instead of resumes

### For Policymakers
1. **Tie WIOA performance metrics to real-time labor market data** — static targets incentivize compliance over impact
2. **Fund regional intelligence platforms** — the cost of a JIE deployment ($25-35K) is trivial compared to the WIOA dollars flowing through each workforce area
